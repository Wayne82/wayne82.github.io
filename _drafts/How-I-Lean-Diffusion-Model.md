It's been 2 months since my last post about coding a toy transformer from scratch, where at the end I mentioned 2 potential trajectories for my next learning direction: one is to continue learning more theoetical knowledge in AI with enough mathematical depth, and the other is to gain more hands-on experience by building some real-world projects with agentic AI. At last, it is still the first one choosing me, which I have been learning another very attractive technology in AI: **diffusion model** in the past 2 months. And in this post, I want to record how I learn diffusion model so far.

## Where I Start
The first time I experienced the "magic" of text to image generation was through the DALL-E from OpenAI and in the Discord group of Midjourney. Though it was very impressive, it didn't trigger my immediate interest, until the Stable Diffusion was released by Stability AI, which also open-sourced the code and model weights. And its the mechanism of creating high-quality images from pure noise really caught my deep curiosity.

This was where I started, and finally I spent most of my spare time in the past 2 months learning the diffusion model.

## The Learning Plan
My learning plan is progressive, including the following steps:
1. Get a conceptual understanding of the diffusion model.
2. Understand the mathematical formulations and the proof behind the scene.
3. Model architecture and how to train a diffusion model.
4. Get hands-on experience by implementing a toy diffusion model from scratch.
5. Know the development history and explore the latest research papers.

At this point, I just reached the second step. Learning the mathematical formulations is really a challenge for me, as I have to spend the time to acquire the necessary mathematical knowledge, such as Probability Theory, Stochastic Differential Equations, Variational Bayesian methods, etc. It won't be a comprehensive course learning, but will reach to the level of enough for me to understand the mathematical explanations for diffusion model.
